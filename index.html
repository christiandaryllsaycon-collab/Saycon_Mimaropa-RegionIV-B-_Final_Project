About MIMAROPA

MIMAROPA, officially known as Region IV-B, is a tourism-rich and culturally diverse region in the Philippines composed of Mindoro (Oriental & Occidental), Marinduque, Romblon, and Palawan. Famous for its ** pristine beaches, world-class diving spots, limestone cliffs, and untouched natural wonders**, MIMAROPA is one of the country’s top destinations for eco-tourism and adventure travel.

The region is home to unique indigenous groups such as the Mangyan, Tagbanua, and Cuyonon, whose traditions, crafts, and rituals add depth to its cultural heritage. From the globally renowned Coron and El Nido in Palawan to the marble craftsmanship of Romblon and the colorful Moriones Festival of Marinduque, MIMAROPA offers travelers a blend of history, culture, and nature.

With its growing tourism industry, sustainable communities, and abundant natural resources, MIMAROPA continues to attract visitors looking for authentic island experiences, cultural immersion, and outdoor adventures in one of the most beautiful regions of the Philippines.
