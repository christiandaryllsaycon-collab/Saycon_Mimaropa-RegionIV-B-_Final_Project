Activities & Experiences

Fun things to do in MIMAROPA

Adventure

Scuba Diving

Scuba diving in Palawan offers some of the clearest waters and richest marine biodiversity in the Philippines. Divers can explore vibrant coral reefs, dramatic underwater cliffs, and historic World War II shipwrecks, especially in Coron. The area is home to sea turtles, reef sharks, colorful fish, and rare marine species. Whether you’re a beginner or an experienced diver, Palawan’s dive spots provide calm conditions, stunning visibility, and unforgettable underwater scenery. It’s one of the top diving destinations in Southeast Asia for adventure and marine conservation.

Island Hopping

Island hopping in Palawan is one of the region’s most popular activities, offering stunning lagoons, white-sand beaches, and crystal-clear turquoise waters. Travelers can explore hidden caves, dramatic limestone cliffs, and vibrant snorkeling spots filled with marine life. Each island has its own unique charm, from serene sandbars to postcard-perfect beaches. The tours are flexible and suitable for all ages, making it a fun and relaxing adventure. Island hopping is the perfect way to experience Palawan’s natural beauty in one unforgettable day.

Hiking

Hiking in Palawan offers breathtaking views of lush forests, limestone mountains, and pristine coastlines. Popular trails like Mount Tapyas and Cleopatra’s Needle give adventurers a mix of challenging climbs and rewarding panoramic scenery. Along the way, hikers can encounter diverse wildlife and unique plant species found only in the region. The trails are well-loved for their natural beauty and peaceful atmosphere. Whether you’re a casual trekker or a seasoned climber, Palawan’s landscapes make every hike unforgettable.

Culture

Attend the Moriones Festivals

Attending the Moriones Festival in Marinduque is a colorful and immersive cultural experience. The festival features locals dressed as Roman soldiers, reenacting biblical scenes and performing lively street processions. Visitors can enjoy traditional music, dances, and community events that showcase the province’s deep religious heritage. The vibrant masks and costumes make the celebration visually striking and unforgettable. It’s a meaningful way to witness faith, history, and artistry come together in one Philippine tradition.

Visit Romblon Marble workshops

Visiting Romblon’s marble workshops gives you a close look at the island’s centuries-old craftsmanship. Skilled artisans carve beautiful sculptures, home décor, and intricate designs from high-quality local marble. Travelers can watch the carving process firsthand, from shaping the stone to polishing the final piece. The workshops also offer unique handmade souvenirs that reflect the province’s rich artistic heritage. It’s an inspiring experience that highlights Romblon’s reputation as the “Marble Capital of the Philippines.”

Explore traditional Palawan villages

xploring traditional Palawan villages offers a glimpse into the indigenous cultures and daily life of communities like the Tagbanua and Palaw’an peoples. Visitors can observe traditional homes, local crafts, and cultural practices that have been preserved for generations. The experience often includes learning about ancestral beliefs, weaving, and sustainable ways of living close to nature. Friendly locals share stories that highlight their deep connection to the land and sea. It’s a meaningful cultural immersion that reveals the heart of Palawan beyond its tourist attractions.

Food

Try local seafood delicacies

Trying local seafood delicacies in Palawan is a must for food lovers, thanks to the island’s fresh catch and rich coastal flavors. Popular dishes include grilled fish, stuffed squid, lobster, and the unique tamilok, all prepared with simple yet delicious local seasonings. Many seaside restaurants serve meals straight from the boat to your plate, guaranteeing freshness. The variety of seafood reflects Palawan’s abundant marine life and vibrant food culture. Every bite offers a taste of the island’s natural bounty and culinary heritage.

Taste Marinduque’s kakanin (rice cakes)

Tasting Marinduque’s kakanin is a delightful experience that showcases the island’s rich culinary traditions. Popular varieties like bibingkang Labo, kalamay-hati, and suman sa lihiya are made from sticky rice, coconut milk, and local ingredients that give each treat its distinct flavor. These rice cakes are often handmade by locals using traditional methods passed down through generations. You can find them in markets, festivals, and small roadside shops, each offering a taste of Marinduque’s warm hospitality. Enjoying these kakanin is a sweet and authentic way to experience the island’s culture.

Experience street food in Puerto Princesa

Experiencing street food in Puerto Princesa lets you enjoy flavorful and affordable local dishes prepared right in front of you. Popular favorites include grilled seafood, chicken inasal, pork barbecue, and adventurous options like tamilok and crocodile sisig. Night markets such as Baywalk offer lively stalls filled with delicious aromas and friendly vendors. The variety of flavors reflects the city’s coastal culture and creative culinary style. It’s a fun and tasty way to explore Puerto Princesa like a local.
